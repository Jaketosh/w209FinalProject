W Shape

The W-shaped curve is marked by a fall into recession followed by a short period of growth, a fall back, and then finally a recovery. This type of fluctuation creates a double-dip shape, or a "W" shape. W-shaped recessions also tend to be more prolonged than V-shaped. And example of a W-shaped curved is The Early 80's Recession in the United States.
